Yishan Wang

I am a first-year PhD candidate at TU Eindhoven, where I work on AI agents for healthcare and multimodal learning.

I received my Master's degree in Cognitive Science from the University of Amsterdam (2024), where I was supervised by Pia Sommerauer and Jelke Bloem. Before that, I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Philosophy from Peking University (2022).

My research interests lie in multimodal learning, large language models, and AI agents for healthcare. I am particularly interested in understanding how language models acquire and exhibit biases, and how human factors like empathy and demographics shape preferences for AI-generated content.

Publications
Your Mileage May Vary: How Empathy and Demographics Shape Human Preferences in LLM Responses
Yishan Wang, Pia Sommerauer, Jelke Bloem
EMNLP 2025 Findings
PDF

We investigate how individual differences in empathy and demographics affect human preferences for LLM-generated responses in emotionally charged scenarios. Our findings reveal significant variations across user groups, challenging the one-size-fits-all approach to LLM alignment.

The Negation Bias in Large Language Models: Investigating bias reflected in linguistic markers
Yishan Wang, Pia Sommerauer, Jelke Bloem
COLM 2025 (Conference on Language Modeling)
OpenReview / PDF

We investigate whether LLMs acquire subtle linguistic biases like negation bias by combining social science theories with NLP research. Using perplexity as a metric, we evaluate eight models and find that autoregressive decoder models exhibit this bias while stacked encoder models do not.
